SEA FACTS

World's largest eye belongs to the squid Architeuthis dux

The eyes of these imposing marine animals can measure up to 30 centimeters in diameter.
This is larger than a basketball, which is about 24 centimeters in diameter.

Reference

At more than 36 meters long, the lion's mane jellyfish - Cyanea capillata is the largest in the world.

These cnidarians inhabit the abyssal depths, so they are generally rarely sighted by humans. Their tentacles vary in color depending on the individual,
although they usually alternate between yellow and orange, hence the similarity with the mane of the king of the jungle.
Their diet consists of small fish, zooplankton and other jellyfish, animals that are trapped in their tentacles that inject a lethal venom.

Reference

Young dolphins like to take drugs, thanks to the toxin produced by pufferfish.

According to a documentary published by BBC Earth on September 19 on Youtube,
a fish and a robot turtle full of cameras are introduced to spy on a group of dolphins
in order to record their activities. The dolphins show behavior that could be interpreted
as euphoric after having ingested the toxins produced by this type of fish.

Fitzroy's tortoises can breathe through their anus.

This is because the drop in temperatures reduces oxygen levels to a minimum due to the freezing of the surface.
For this reason, some animals such as turtles have to lend themselves to numerous strategies in order to adapt to this type of
hibernation process. First of all, the body's functions are slowed down, thus reducing the amount of oxygen needed to live.
